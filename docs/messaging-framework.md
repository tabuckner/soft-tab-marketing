## Objection Handling (FAQ Content)

| Objection | Response Direction |
|---|---|
| "You're just one person, not a team" | We bring in specialized subcontractors as needed. You get a senior lead (me) plus the right specialists for your project. Every engagement has a consistent point of contact who owns the outcome. |
| "How is this different from hiring a freelancer?" | Freelancers execute tasks. We own outcomes. We bring strategic thinking, architecture decisions, and AI-augmented workflows ‚Äî not just hours of coding. |
| "What if AI-generated code is low quality?" | AI is a tool, not a replacement for engineering judgment. We use AI to accelerate the repetitive parts while our senior engineers make every architectural and quality decision. The result is faster delivery with the same (or higher) quality bar. |
| "What technologies do you work with?" | We're stack-flexible ‚Äî [list primary technologies]. We choose the right tool for your problem, not force-fit our favorite framework. |
| "How much does this cost?" | Every project is different. We offer free discovery calls to understand your needs and provide a clear proposal. Typical engagements range from short-term advisory to multi-month builds. |
| "Can you work with our existing team?" | Absolutely. Team augmentation is one of our core offerings. We embed seamlessly into your workflow, tools, and processes. |

## Writing Style Guide (Quick Reference)

- **Voice**: Confident, technical, approachable
- **Person**: "We" (not "I" ‚Äî reinforces team positioning)
- **Audience address**: "You" / "Your" (second person, direct)
- **Sentences**: Mix short and long. Lead with short.
- **Jargon**: Use technical terms correctly but explain when needed
- **Avoid**: "Leverage," "synergy," "paradigm," "bleeding-edge," "disrupt"
- **CTA language**: Action verbs ‚Äî "Schedule," "Start," "Build," "Talk" (not "Submit," "Click here")
